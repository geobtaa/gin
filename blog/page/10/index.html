    By Karen Majewicz

    The Minnesota Geospatial Commons was officially launched in 2015, and serves as the state’s central clearinghouse for public geospatial data. The site runs on an open source application (CKAN) and is operated by the Minnesota Geospatial Information Office (MnGeo). The Commons has grown from about 200 unique datasets at the time of public launch to over 800 today.
