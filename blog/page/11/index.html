    Project Highlights

    BTAA GDP Monthly Report - July 2020

    News

    First collection from Nebraska added

    For Nebraska first contribution\, we harvested over 100 new geospatial data records from NebraskaMap\, the state's open data portal. There are many fun datasets and themes in this collection\, including the Cowboy Trail and several types of wildlife (Antelope\, Elk\, Bighorn Sheep\, Mountain Lions\, and more).

    First Data Provider Interview

    We published our first of a series of Data Provider Interviews\, posts designed to highlight local governments and institutions that offer open GIS data. In each interview\, providers tell us about their missions and data resources.

    This interview was with Dan Seidensticker from Madison\, WI. He described his work in a public agency and how having their data available in other geoportals has saved him time while bringing more exposure to it.

    Metadata: Workflow & Schema Developments

    Monthly DCAT record harvests

    Our new Graduate Research Assistant made several improvements to our DCAT harvesting code and process. This process involves monthly re-accessions 64 open data portals that regularly publish new records and retire old ones.

    We are now developing more detailed step-by-step workflow documentation with the goal of sharing it across the BTAA and beyond.

    GeoBlacklight Schema 2.0

    In preparation for the upcoming GeoBlacklight Community Summer Sprint\, we have gathered several issues around metadata that the growing community is hoping to address. These include:

    More techniques for defining relationships between items

    Alignment of various custom fields that could be merged into the official schema

    Strategies for uncertainty in dates

    Incorporating access and usage rights

    Geoportal Tip: Opening a dataset in ArcGIS Online

    By Karen Majewicz If you are looking to perform geospatial analysis on a dataset in the geoportal, using the “Open in ArcGIS” button will come in handy. A large percentage of the geospatial data resources in the geoportal are also available as ArcGIS Rest Services, which are hosted streaming datasets that can be added to an online map application. To find the resources with web services, use the facet “Type” and select “Service”.
